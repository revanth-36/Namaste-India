Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh, located in central India, is a state known for its rich tribal culture, ancient heritage, and stunning natural beauty. Its capital city of Raipur serves as a gateway to the state's many attractions. Chhattisgarh is home to a wealth of archaeological wonders, including the UNESCO-listed rock-cut temples of Sirpur and the ancient caves of Kanger Valley National Park, adorned with exquisite rock art. The state's lush forests, rolling hills, and pristine rivers offer opportunities for eco-tourism, trekking, and wildlife safaris, with destinations like Bastar and Surguja providing glimpses into tribal life and traditions. Chhattisgarh's vibrant culture is celebrated through its colorful festivals, traditional dance forms like Pandwani and Raut Nacha, and mouthwatering cuisine, which includes iconic dishes like chila, fara, and petha. Whether exploring its historic landmarks, immersing in its natural beauty, or experiencing its vibrant culture, Chhattisgarh promises a journey filled with warmth, discovery, and unforgettable experiences.

Chitrakoot Waterfall

The Chitrakoot Waterfall, also known as the "Niagara of India," is a breathtaking natural wonder located in the Bastar district of Chhattisgarh, India. It is one of the widest waterfalls in India and is formed by the Indravati River cascading over a horseshoe-shaped cliff, creating a stunning vista of foaming white water surrounded by lush greenery. The Chitrakoot Waterfall is nestled amidst the dense forests of the Bastar region, making it a picturesque and serene destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike. Visitors can enjoy scenic hikes, picnics, and photography sessions amidst the pristine beauty of the waterfall and its surroundings. The area around the waterfall is also home to diverse flora and fauna, adding to its ecological significance. The Chitrakoot Waterfall is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking to experience the raw beauty and tranquility of nature in the heart of India.

Bhoramdeo Temple

The Bhoramdeo Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in the Bhoramdeo village of Chhattisgarh, India. Believed to have been built in the 11th century by the Nag dynasty rulers, the temple complex is renowned for its intricate carvings, architectural elegance, and spiritual significance. The temple is often referred to as the "Khajuraho of Chhattisgarh" due to the similarity in architectural style and the presence of erotic sculptures on its walls. The Bhoramdeo Temple complex consists of several shrines dedicated to various Hindu deities, including Lord Shiva, Lord Vishnu, and Goddess Saraswati. The main temple is adorned with elaborate carvings depicting scenes from Hindu mythology, celestial beings, and intricate patterns. The temple complex is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a serene and tranquil atmosphere for devotees and visitors alike. The Bhoramdeo Temple is not only a place of worship but also a significant cultural and historical landmark, attracting tourists and pilgrims from across the country.

Chitrakote Falls

The Chitrakote Falls, also known as the "Niagara of India," is a spectacular waterfall located in the Bastar district of Chhattisgarh, India. It is the widest waterfall in India and is formed by the Indravati River cascading down a horseshoe-shaped cliff, creating a mesmerizing sight of foaming white water surrounded by lush greenery. The Chitrakote Falls is nestled amidst dense forests, offering a picturesque and serene setting for visitors to immerse themselves in nature's beauty. The best time to visit the falls is during the monsoon season, when the water flow is at its peak, creating a majestic spectacle of cascading water. Visitors can enjoy boat rides to get a closer view of the waterfall and explore the surrounding area, which is home to diverse flora and fauna. The Chitrakote Falls is a popular tourist destination and a must-visit for anyone seeking to experience the raw power and natural splendor of India's waterfalls.

Ambuja City Center Mall

Situated in the heart of the city, the mall offers a wide range of retail outlets, dining options, and entertainment facilities, making it a favorite spot for locals and visitors alike. The Ambuja City Centre Mall also hosts a range of entertainment options, such as a multiplex cinema, gaming zones, and indoor amusement rides, providing entertainment for people of all ages. With its modern amenities and vibrant atmosphere, the mall offers a comprehensive shopping and leisure experience for the entire family.

Magneto - The Mall

Magneto The Mall is a popular shopping mall located in Raipur, Chhattisgarh, India. It is one of the largest and most prominent shopping destinations in the state. The mall offers a wide range of shopping options, including clothing stores, electronics shops, accessories outlets, and more. Naya Raipur Central Park

Naya Raipur Central Park, located in the heart of Naya Raipur, Chhattisgarh, is a sprawling urban oasis offering a serene retreat from the city's hustle and bustle. Spanning across vast green expanses, the park features lush lawns, verdant gardens, and scenic landscapes, providing an ideal setting for relaxation and recreation. Visitors can explore well-maintained walking and jogging tracks, meander along the tranquil lake, or enjoy boating amidst picturesque surroundings. Children can frolic in designated play areas equipped with swings and slides, while cultural enthusiasts can attend performances at the amphitheater or admire the park's sculptures and art installations. Dedicated zones for yoga and meditation offer peaceful retreats for spiritual practice and relaxation, while cycling tracks cater to fitness enthusiasts. Naya Raipur Central Park stands as a popular destination for residents and tourists alike, offering a harmonious blend of natural beauty, recreational amenities, and cultural experiences.

Shri Rajiv Lochan Mandir

Shri Rajeev Lochan Mandir is a revered Hindu temple situated in Raipur, the capital city of Chhattisgarh, India. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, the temple holds significant religious importance among devotees. It is renowned for its spiritual ambiance, architectural grandeur, and vibrant festivals celebrated throughout the year. The temple's sanctum sanctorum houses the idol of Lord Vishnu in the form of Rajeev Lochan, attracting devotees who seek blessings for prosperity, well-being, and spiritual fulfillment. The temple's serene surroundings and peaceful atmosphere provide a tranquil retreat for meditation and prayer. Devotees and visitors often gather here to participate in various rituals, offer prayers, and immerse themselves in the divine presence of Lord Vishnu. Shri Rajeev Lochan Mandir stands as a symbol of faith and devotion, drawing pilgrims and seekers from far and wide to experience its sacredness and grace.

Tirathgarh Falls

Tirathgarh Falls is a picturesque waterfall located near the Kanger Valley National Park in Bastar district of Chhattisgarh, India. It is one of the most scenic waterfalls in the state, cascading from a height of approximately 300 feet (91 meters) amidst lush greenery and rocky cliffs. The waterfall forms a natural pool at the base, offering visitors a refreshing spot to swim and relax. Surrounding the waterfall are dense forests inhabited by diverse flora and fauna, adding to its natural beauty and ecological significance. Tirathgarh Falls is a popular tourist destination and is best visited during the monsoon season when the water flow is at its peak, creating a magnificent spectacle of cascading water. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the waterfall, explore the surrounding nature trails, and experience the tranquility of the pristine wilderness.

Ghatarani Waterfalls

Ghatarani Waterfalls is a scenic waterfall located near Raipur in the state of Chhattisgarh, India. Situated amidst lush green forests and rocky cliffs, the waterfall is a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Ghatarani Waterfalls cascades down from a height, creating a serene and picturesque setting. Visitors can enjoy the natural beauty of the surroundings, take a refreshing dip in the cool waters, or simply relax and unwind amidst the tranquil ambiance. The area around Ghatarani Waterfalls is also known for its rich biodiversity, offering opportunities for bird watching and nature walks. The waterfall is best visited during the monsoon season when the water flow is at its peak, creating a breathtaking spectacle. However, it is essential to exercise caution and follow safety guidelines while visiting the waterfall, especially during the rainy season when the paths may be slippery.

Shahid Veer Narayan Singh International Cricket Stadium

The Shahid Veer Narayan Singh International Cricket Stadium is a cricket stadium located in Raipur, the capital city of Chhattisgarh, India. It is named in honor of Veer Narayan Singh, a prominent freedom fighter from the region. The stadium is one of the premier cricket venues in Central India and has hosted several domestic and international cricket matches since its inauguration in 2008. With a seating capacity of around 65,000 spectators, the stadium boasts modern facilities and amenities for players and spectators alike. It has hosted matches in various formats, including Test matches, One Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 Internationals (T20Is). The stadium's floodlights enable day-night matches to be played, adding to its versatility as a cricketing venue. The Shahid Veer Narayan Singh International Cricket Stadium serves as a hub for cricket enthusiasts in Chhattisgarh and has played a significant role in promoting the sport in the region.

Kevalya Dham Jain Temple

Kevalya Dham is a Jain temple located in Raipur, Chhattisgarh, India. It is a renowned pilgrimage site for followers of Jainism and attracts devotees from across the country. The temple complex is dedicated to Lord Adinath, the first Tirthankara of Jainism, and features an impressive marble idol of the deity adorned with intricate carvings and decorations. Kevalya Dham is known for its peaceful ambiance, spiritual atmosphere, and architectural beauty, making it a popular destination for meditation, prayer, and religious ceremonies. The temple complex also includes facilities for accommodation, dining, and other amenities to cater to the needs of visitors and pilgrims. Kevalya Dham holds special significance for Jain devotees and serves as a place of reverence and worship in the Jain tradition.

MM Fun City is known for its vibrant atmosphere, lively entertainment, and exciting rides, making it a popular leisure destination in Raipur.
